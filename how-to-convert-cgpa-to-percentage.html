How to Convert CGPA to Percentage – All Formulas Explained

Published on: 21/03/2026

Whether you use a CGPA Calculator or prefer working it out by hand, understanding the conversion from CGPA to percentage is essential. Job applications, scholarship forms, visa documents, and university admission portals almost always ask for a percentage — even if your university only gives you a CGPA.

The tricky part is that there is no single universal formula. The conversion depends on which grading scale your institution uses — 4.0, 10.0, 5.0, or another. If you are unsure which scale your CGPA is on, first use the GPA ↔ CGPA converter to identify and align your score.

Why Is There Not Just One Simple Formula?

On a 10-point scale, a grade of 10 does not necessarily mean you scored 100% on every exam. Universities typically use grade bands — scoring between 90% and 100% might all be graded as a 10. Because of this compression, you cannot just multiply by 10 and call it a percentage. You need a specific multiplier that accounts for this adjustment.

📌 Key Takeaway: CGPA scales compress raw percentage marks into grade point ranges. Each conversion formula is designed to undo that compression and give an approximate percentage equivalent.

Formula 1 - The 10-Point Scale (CBSE Standard)

Percentage = CGPA × 9.5

Why 9.5 and Not 10?

A CGPA of 10 on the 10-point scale represents the highest possible grade band — not a perfect 100% in raw marks. The 9.5 multiplier is calibrated to reflect that reality more accurately.

Worked Examples — 10-Point Scale (CBSE)

  • CGPA of 6.0 → 6.0 × 9.5 = 57.0%
  • CGPA of 7.5 → 7.5 × 9.5 = 71.25%
  • CGPA of 8.4 → 8.4 × 9.5 = 79.8%
  • CGPA of 9.2 → 9.2 × 9.5 = 87.4%

Formula 2 - The 4.0 Scale (US and Global Standard)

Percentage = (CGPA ÷ 4.0) × 100

Worked Examples — 4.0 Scale

  • CGPA of 2.5 → (2.5 ÷ 4.0) × 100 = 62.5%
  • CGPA of 3.0 → (3.0 ÷ 4.0) × 100 = 75.0%
  • CGPA of 3.6 → (3.6 ÷ 4.0) × 100 = 90.0%
  • CGPA of 3.9 → (3.9 ÷ 4.0) × 100 = 97.5%

⚠️ Important: This formula is a linear estimate. Different US universities may use slightly different percentage bands for the same grade point.

Formula 3 - AICTE Standard (Technical Education in India)

Percentage = (CGPA - 0.75) × 10

Worked Examples — AICTE

  • CGPA of 7.0 → (7.0 - 0.75) × 10 = 62.5%
  • CGPA of 8.0 → (8.0 - 0.75) × 10 = 72.5%
  • CGPA of 8.75 → (8.75 - 0.75) × 10 = 80.0%
  • CGPA of 9.5 → (9.5 - 0.75) × 10 = 87.5%

📌 If you studied engineering, pharmacy, MBA, or another AICTE-regulated programme in India, use this formula rather than the standard CBSE 9.5 multiplier.

Critical Steps Before Using Any Conversion for Official Purposes

  1. Check your official transcript for the exact authorised formula.
  2. Follow the instructions from the recipient (university/job portal).
  3. Do not round up unless instructed; round to two decimals.
  4. Use credential evaluators like WES for international applications.
  5. Contact your university registrar if in doubt for official conversion.

Common Mistakes Students Make

  • Multiplying every CGPA by 9.5 regardless of scale.
  • Assuming CGPA × 10 is always correct.
  • Using the same formula for all programmes — AICTE vs CBSE matters.

Quick Summary — All Formulas at a Glance

  • 10-Point Scale (CBSE / Standard): Percentage = CGPA × 9.5
  • 4.0 Scale (US / Global): Percentage = (CGPA ÷ 4.0) × 100
  • AICTE (Technical India): Percentage = (CGPA - 0.75) × 10
  • European ECTS: Based on cohort ranking, not a multiplier
  • Canada / Australia: Use institutional lookup tables

🎓 Final Thought: Converting CGPA to percentage sounds simple, but doing it correctly takes care. Use the right formula, follow instructions, and get an official document when needed.
