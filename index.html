Knowledge Base

What is CGPA?

CGPA stands for Cumulative Grade Point Average. It shows your total academic performance in one number.

You calculate CGPA from the grade points you get in all subjects, along with their credit hours. Each subject has a grade like A, B, or C, and each grade has a fixed point value. These points are combined and averaged to form your final CGPA.

Unlike GPA, which shows your performance in a single semester, CGPA shows your performance across all semesters. It gives a complete view of how you performed throughout your study program.

CGPA is your final average score based on all subjects you have studied.

Calculation Method

CGPA Formula

The CGPA formula takes into account both the grade you earned and the number of credits each course carries. Higher-credit courses have more influence on your final CGPA than lower-credit ones.

CGPA =
∑ (Grade Points × Credits) across all Semesters Total Credits Attempted across all Semesters

For example, if you earned a 4.00 in a 3-credit course and a 3.00 in a 2-credit course, your GPA for those courses would be: (4.00×3 + 3.00×2) ÷ (3+2) = 18 ÷ 5 = 3.60. This same logic is applied cumulatively across all semesters.

Comparison

CGPA vs GPA

Students often confuse CGPA and GPA — while they are closely related, they measure different things. Here's a clear breakdown of the key differences:

AspectGPA (Grade Point Average)CGPA (Cumulative GPA)
ScopeSingle semester or termEntire academic program
Also Known AsSGPA (Semester GPA)Overall / Cumulative GPA
Calculation PeriodOne term at a timeAll completed semesters
Use CaseTracking semester performanceScholarships, jobs, grad school
Resets Each Semester?Yes — fresh each termNo — running average
Impact of Bad SemesterOnly affects that semesterLowers your overall CGPA
Impact of Good SemesterDoesn't fix past semestersGradually improves CGPA

GPA tells you how you did this semester, while CGPA tells you how you've done throughout your entire degree. Both matter — a strong CGPA requires consistent performance across all semesters, not just a few good ones.

CGPA to percentage conversion is not universal — each institution follows its own method. The most common formula used in India (CBSE) is: Percentage = CGPA × 9.5. For US 4.0 scale: Percentage = (CGPA ÷ 4.0) × 100. Always verify with your institution's official guidelines.
