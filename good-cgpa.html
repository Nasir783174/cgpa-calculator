What Is a Good CGPA? Benchmarks for Jobs, Grad School & Scholarships

Published on: 27/03/2026

When you calculate CGPA, the number you get only tells half the story. The other half is knowing what that number actually means for your goals — whether you are aiming for a job at a top company, applying to graduate school, or simply trying to stay eligible for a scholarship.

The honest answer to 'what is a good CGPA?' is this: it depends entirely on what you want to do next. A 3.2 on a 4.0 scale might be perfectly fine for one path and not enough for another.

What Is a Good CGPA on the 4.0 Scale?

CGPA RangeLevelStandingWhat It Unlocks
3.8 – 4.0ExceptionalTop of ClassIvy League grad school, medical school, elite law firms

🏆 The Sweet Spot: On a 4.0 scale, a CGPA between 3.5 and 3.7 is widely considered the most practical target. It clears most automated filters and qualifies you for competitive programmes.

🏆 The Sweet Spot: On a 10.0 scale, an 8.0 CGPA is the widely recognised benchmark. Most top tech companies set their internal screening filters at 8.0.

What If Your CGPA Is Lower Than the Target?

1. A Rising Trajectory Is a Powerful Signal

If your CGPA started low but has been climbing steadily each semester, that upward trend tells a story of growth and self-improvement. If your overall CGPA is lower than ideal but your recent semesters have been strong, highlight your recent semester GPAs prominently on your resume.

2. Your Major GPA Can Speak Louder Than Your Overall CGPA

If your grades in your core major subjects are significantly higher than your overall CGPA, you can present your Major GPA separately. If your overall CGPA is 3.1 but your Major GPA in Computer Science is 3.8, a technical employer will care far more about that 3.8. Use the Grade Calculator to find your subject-wise grades and predict your Major GPA.

3. Skills, Projects, and Portfolios

In fields like software development, data science, and design, a strong portfolio of real work often carries more weight than a number on a transcript.

4. Certifications and Standardised Test Scores

A strong GRE, GMAT, or relevant professional certification can help offset a lower CGPA for graduate school applications.

The Gold Standard — What Should Every Student Aim For?

ScaleGold Standard CGPAWhat This Level Keeps Open
4.0 Scale3.5Passes most Fortune 500 filters, qualifies for competitive grad school, keeps scholarship options open
10.0 Scale8.0Clears top tech company filters (Google, Microsoft), qualifies for most campus placement drives and PSU roles

Quick Summary

  1. On a 4.0 scale: 3.0 is good, 3.5 is very competitive, and 3.8+ is exceptional.
  2. On a 10.0 scale: 7.0 is the baseline, 8.0 is the sweet spot, and 9.0+ is outstanding.
  3. The right target depends on your industry.
  4. A rising trend in recent semesters can offset a lower overall CGPA.
  5. Your Major GPA, skills portfolio, and certifications all strengthen your profile.

🎓 Final Thought: A good CGPA is not about perfection — it is about being good enough for the opportunities you are chasing, while building the rest of your profile to back it up.
