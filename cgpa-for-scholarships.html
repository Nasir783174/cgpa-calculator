CGPA for Scholarships – Minimum Requirements by Program & Scale

Published on: 26/03/2026

Why Your CGPA Is the First Thing Scholarship Boards Look At

Before any scholarship committee reads your essay or checks your recommendations, an automated grade point calculator or screening system checks your CGPA. In today's high-volume application environment, your academic score is the very first filter.

Scholarship boards receive thousands of applications every cycle. They sort by CGPA first, shortlist the top candidates, and only then begin reviewing essays, references, and personal statements. If your CGPA does not clear the threshold, the rest of your application may never be seen by a human at all.

Scholarship Highlights by Programme

DAAD Scholarship — Germany

DAAD requires a 'First Class' degree equivalent — minimum 8.5 on a 10.0 scale or 3.5 on a 4.0 scale. Use the GPA to CGPA converter to check your equivalent score. Two or more years of relevant work experience can offset a slightly lower CGPA.

Commonwealth Scholarships — UK

Minimum Upper Second Class Honours (2:1) is needed — around 3.3 GPA or 7.5 CGPA. For Shared Scholarships, successful candidates often hold a First Class, i.e., 3.7+ or 8.5+.

Fulbright Scholarship — USA

Official minimum is 3.0 GPA, but most successful applicants present 3.5 or higher. Leadership, community involvement, and project strength are as important as CGPA.

Canadian Excellence Scholarships

Tier 1 requires ~3.9 GPA (90%+), Tier 2 requires ~3.5–3.8 GPA (80–89.9%). Accurate conversion is essential.

Quick Summary

  1. Elite / Full-Funded: 3.7+ (4.0) or 9.0+ (10.0).
  2. Merit-Based Partial: 3.3–3.6 (4.0) or 8.0–8.9 (10.0).
  3. General Eligibility: 3.0 (4.0) or 7.0–7.5 (10.0).
  4. Holistic profile (tests, SOP, experience) can compensate for slightly lower CGPA.
  5. Always check AI screening — know whether cutoffs are hard or recommended.

🎓 Final Thought: Scholarships are awarded to the most complete, compelling candidate — not just the highest CGPA. Your CGPA opens the door; your full profile determines if you get chosen.
