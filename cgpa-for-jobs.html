Does CGPA Matter for Jobs? The Truth in 2026

Published on: 25/03/2026

The Short Answer - Yes, But Not in the Way You Think

Yes, CGPA matters for a job. In 2026, your CGPA acts as a gatekeeper for your first opportunity. It is the filter that decides whether your resume even gets read by a human being. But once you are inside the room, your skills, your personality, and your track record take over entirely.

Think of it as an entry ticket to a concert. The ticket gets you through the gate. But what happens inside depends entirely on what you bring with you.

📌 The Core Truth of 2026: CGPA determines where your career begins. Skills determine how far it goes. Both matter — but at different stages.

The Big Shift — How Much Has CGPA's Role Changed?

In 2019, ~73% of employers used CGPA as a formal screening tool. By early 2026, that number dropped to ~42% — a dramatic shift driven by skills-first hiring. However, CGPA still matters for first-time job seekers, legacy industries, and high-volume recruitment.

The Screening Tool Reality — Automated Filters

Large companies use ATS (Applicant Tracking Systems) that automatically filter out candidates below preset CGPA thresholds:

  • 3.0 on a 4.0 scale for US/international companies
  • 7.0 on a 10.0 scale for Indian/Asian companies — convert between GPA and CGPA scales here
  • 2:1 (Upper Second Class) for UK-based employers

⚠️ Practical Warning: Always check job postings for minimum CGPA requirements — treat them as hard entry thresholds when stated.

Quick Summary

  1. Yes — CGPA is still used as an automated filter by ~42% of employers in 2026.
  2. Matters most for first jobs, campus placements, government roles, and legacy industries.
  3. Matters least for startups, remote work, creative industries, and skills-first hiring.
  4. Common minimum thresholds: 3.0 (4.0) / 7.0 (10.0).
  5. CGPA expires after 2–3 years of professional experience.
  6. Low CGPA can be offset with portfolios, certifications, internships, and referrals.

🎓 Final Thought: CGPA is your entry ticket — it gets you through the gate. Your performance, skills, and relationships determine how far you go. Earn the best CGPA you can, but make sure everything else you build is even stronger.
